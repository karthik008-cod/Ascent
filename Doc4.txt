# ASCENT — Gameplay Bible (Version 1.0)

---

# Core Gameplay Loop

Every day follows the same simple loop:

1. Open Ascent.
2. Visit your companion at the current campsite.
3. Plan today's expedition by choosing tasks.
4. Complete tasks in real life.
5. Watch your companion climb after each completed task.
6. Reach today's campsite.
7. Relax and prepare for tomorrow.

Simple to understand.

Deep to experience.

---

# Daily Expedition

Every day generates one expedition.

An expedition is today's journey.

It has:

* A starting campsite
* A destination campsite
* A beautiful trail
* Scenic moments along the way

The destination is always reachable.

No impossible days.

---

# Tasks

Tasks are not "todos."

They are promises to yourself.

Examples:

* Workout
* Study Cryptography
* Build Ascent
* Read 20 pages
* Drink water
* Call parents
* Practice Flutter

Every completed promise becomes one step forward.

---

# Completing a Task

When a task is marked complete:

The companion:

* Smiles gently.
* Takes a meaningful step.
* The environment subtly reacts.
* The completed section of the trail becomes part of the journey forever.

Completion should feel satisfying without becoming flashy.

---

# Daily Completion

If every task is completed:

The companion reaches camp.

Builds the tent.

Lights the fire.

Places the backpack down.

Looks at the sunset.

The day ends peacefully.

---

# Partial Completion

If only some tasks are completed:

The companion camps wherever they reached.

No disappointment.

No warning.

Tomorrow simply continues from there.

---

# Missed Days

Missing one day...

One week...

Or even one month...

Changes nothing.

The companion simply waits.

The first screen says:

"Welcome back."

No streak loss.

No punishment.

No guilt.

---

# Progress

Progress is measured through:

* Distance climbed
* Altitude reached
* Memories collected
* Regions explored
* Journal entries
* Personal growth

Never by competing with others.

---

# Milestones

Occasionally, the mountain celebrates progress.

Examples:

* Reach a waterfall.
* Cross a suspension bridge.
* Enter a new forest.
* Witness the first snowfall.
* Watch sunrise above the clouds.

Milestones are experiences.

Not trophies.

---

# Discoveries

While climbing, users occasionally discover:

* Hidden caves
* Scenic viewpoints
* Rare flowers
* Ancient journals
* Crystal formations
* Forgotten campsites

These discoveries make every expedition feel unique.

---

# Memories

Every meaningful moment becomes a memory.

Examples:

"The first snowfall."

"The longest expedition."

"The day you finished your exams."

"The sunrise above the clouds."

These memories become part of the user's journey.

---

# Rest Days

Sometimes growth means resting.

Users may intentionally mark a day as:

Rest Day

The companion stays at camp.

Reads.

Drinks coffee.

Watches nature.

The app celebrates healthy recovery.

---

# Long Tasks

Large goals can be divided into stages.

Example:

Build Ascent

□ Research

□ Design

□ Flutter

□ Testing

□ Release

Each completed stage advances the expedition.

---

# Habits

Habits are recurring promises.

Examples:

Exercise

Meditation

Reading

Learning

Calling family

Each occurrence contributes naturally to the journey.

No pressure to maintain streaks.

---

# Goals

Long-term goals exist above daily tasks.

Examples:

Become a Flutter Developer

Learn Cybersecurity

Read 20 Books

Run a Marathon

Daily promises slowly build these larger dreams.

---

# Reflection

Every evening, the user is invited—not forced—to answer:

"What made today meaningful?"

The answer becomes part of the personal journal.

---

# Weekly Reflection

At the end of each week:

The companion revisits the week's journey.

The app highlights:

Places visited.

Promises kept.

Memories made.

The focus is reflection, not statistics.

---

# Monthly Reflection

At the end of every month:

The user sees:

The path climbed.

New regions explored.

Journal highlights.

Beautiful moments.

The goal is to appreciate progress over time.

---

# Failure

Ascent has no failure state.

Only different amounts of progress.

Every step counts.

---

# Competition

There are:

No leaderboards.

No rankings.

No comparisons.

The only mountain that matters is your own.

---

# Rewards

Rewards are emotional rather than material.

Examples:

A sunrise.

A hidden meadow.

A peaceful campsite.

A journal entry.

A new memory.

A breathtaking viewpoint.

The experience is the reward.

---

# Ultimate Goal

The objective is not to finish the mountain.

The objective is to enjoy climbing it.

---

# Gameplay Principles

Every mechanic must answer "Yes" to these questions:

Does it encourage consistency?

Does it reduce guilt?

Does it feel meaningful?

Does it respect the user's pace?

Does it strengthen the emotional bond with the companion?

Does it make the journey more beautiful?

If the answer to any question is "No,"

the feature does not belong in Ascent.
