North Star Document (Version 1)

This is the document that every future design decision, feature, animation, and line of code must respect.

1. What is Ascent?

Ascent is a self-improvement journey disguised as an adventure.

It transforms daily tasks into meaningful progress by letting users climb an endless mountain alongside a silent companion. Instead of rewarding productivity with points and streaks, Ascent rewards consistency with memories, exploration, and a growing world.

The goal is not to make users complete more tasks.

The goal is to help users enjoy becoming the person they want to be.

2. Who is Ascent for?

Ascent is built for people who:

Start goals with excitement but struggle to stay consistent.
Feel guilty after missing a day in traditional habit trackers.
Want motivation without pressure.
Prefer meaningful progress over meaningless numbers.
Love games, exploration, and beautiful experiences.
Want a calmer, healthier relationship with productivity.

Ascent is NOT for users who only want a checklist.

3. What problem does Ascent solve?

Today's productivity apps often create pressure.

Users chase streaks, points, and perfect days.

One missed day feels like failure.

Eventually, they stop opening the app.

Ascent solves this by replacing pressure with progress.

Instead of asking:

"Did you finish everything?"

Ascent asks:

"How far did we climb today?"

Every step matters.

Every day matters.

No day is wasted.

4. What should users feel?

Every session should leave the user feeling:

🌄 Inspired
💪 Motivated
🌱 Hopeful
😌 Peaceful
❤️ Proud of today's effort
✨ Excited for tomorrow

Users should never feel:

Guilty
Ashamed
Punished
Overwhelmed
Pressured
5. Product Principles

Every feature must satisfy these principles.

Principle 1

The mountain never judges.

Principle 2

Progress is always visible.

Principle 3

Celebrate effort, not perfection.

Principle 4

The companion is a friend, not a coach.

Principle 5

The world should feel alive.

Principle 6

The journey is more important than the destination.

Principle 7

Customization tells a story, not a status.

Principle 8

Beauty should never reduce simplicity.

Principle 9

Every animation should have meaning.

Nothing moves just because it looks cool.

Every movement reinforces progress.

Principle 10

Ascent should feel like returning home.

Opening the app should feel like visiting your companion after a long day.

6. Success Statement

If someone uses Ascent every day for one year, we want them to say:

"Ascent didn't make me more productive. It changed the way I think about progress."

7. One Sentence

If Ascent had to be described in one sentence:

"Climb your own mountain, one meaningful step at a time."
