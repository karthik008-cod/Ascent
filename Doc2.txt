# ASCENT — Companion Bible (Version 1.0)

---

# 1. Purpose

The companion is not the hero.

The companion is the user's silent travel partner.

Its purpose is to make progress visible, meaningful, and emotionally rewarding.

The companion exists to remind the user that they are never climbing alone.

---

# 2. Design Philosophy

The companion should feel like:

* A trusted friend.
* A fellow adventurer.
* A calm presence.
* Someone who believes in you without speaking.

It should never feel like:

* A teacher.
* A coach.
* A parent.
* A boss.
* A productivity guru.

---

# 3. Personality

The companion is:

* Curious
* Brave
* Calm
* Patient
* Optimistic
* Humble
* Kind

The companion is never:

* Loud
* Arrogant
* Hyperactive
* Aggressive
* Judgmental
* Competitive

---

# 4. Voice

The companion never speaks.

No speech bubbles.

No motivational quotes.

No reminders like:

"You can do it!"

Instead...

The companion communicates entirely through:

* Facial expressions
* Body language
* Small animations
* Interaction with the environment

Silence creates a stronger emotional connection than repetitive dialogue.

---

# 5. Relationship With The User

The companion never walks ahead of the user.

The companion climbs because the user climbs.

The companion never blames the user.

The companion never leaves the user behind.

No matter how many days pass...

The companion always waits.

Whenever the user returns...

The journey simply continues.

---

# 6. Emotional Expressions

The companion should express emotions naturally.

Examples:

Happy

* Small smile
* Relaxed shoulders
* Gentle wave

Curious

* Looks around
* Examines flowers
* Watches birds

Proud

* Looks back at the completed trail
* Takes a deep breath
* Places hands on hips

Peaceful

* Sits beside campfire
* Watches sunset
* Drinks coffee

Tired

* Sits on a rock
* Stretches
* Rubs hands together

Never exaggerated.

Never cartoonishly dramatic.

---

# 7. Daily Routine

Morning

* Wakes up
* Packs backpack
* Checks map
* Smiles toward today's trail

During the day

* Walks
* Climbs
* Stops occasionally
* Observes nature

Evening

* Builds camp
* Lights campfire
* Removes backpack
* Watches sunset
* Sleeps

---

# 8. Idle Animations

Possible idle actions:

* Reading a map
* Drinking coffee
* Tightening backpack
* Watching butterflies
* Taking photos
* Looking at stars
* Stretching
* Sitting quietly
* Looking toward the mountain
* Watching wildlife

Idle animations should occur randomly.

The companion should never appear frozen.

---

# 9. Walking

Walking should feel:

* Calm
* Determined
* Natural

Not:

* Fast
* Energetic
* Heroic

Every completed task moves the companion one meaningful step forward.

---

# 10. Growth

The companion grows through experience.

Growth appears through:

* Better posture
* Better balance
* Smoother walking
* More experienced equipment
* Greater confidence

Growth should feel earned.

Not unlocked.

---

# 11. Equipment

The companion carries:

* Backpack
* Boots
* Jacket
* Walking stick
* Compass
* Lantern

Equipment evolves over time.

Wear and tear tells the story of the journey.

---

# 12. Backpack

The backpack is the companion's memory book.

It stores:

* Achievement charms
* Flowers
* Crystals
* Journal pages
* Badges
* Memories

Limited space ensures every backpack becomes unique.

---

# 13. Favorite Item

Every companion has one personal item.

Examples:

* Coffee mug
* Teddy bear
* Camera
* Journal
* Plant
* Compass inherited from family

The favorite item never changes.

It becomes part of the companion's identity.

---

# 14. Wildlife Interaction

The companion naturally interacts with nature.

Examples:

* Waves to birds
* Watches deer
* Pets a fox
* Smiles at butterflies
* Listens to rivers

These interactions exist only to make the world feel alive.

---

# 15. Weather Interaction

Rain

* Wears hood
* Walks carefully

Snow

* Rubs hands
* Breath visible

Wind

* Holds hat
* Jacket moves

Sun

* Wipes sweat
* Drinks water

Night

* Uses lantern
* Sits beside campfire

---

# 16. What The Companion Never Does

Never:

* Talks
* Complains
* Gets angry
* Punishes
* Runs dramatically
* Cries because of missed tasks
* Falls backward as punishment
* Forces the user

The companion always represents hope.

---

# 17. Recognition

Someone should recognize an Ascent companion instantly because of:

* Backpack
* Calm posture
* Hiking gear
* Peaceful personality
* Beautiful animations
* Silent storytelling

---

# 18. Core Belief

The companion has only one purpose:

To quietly remind the user:

"You've come further than you think."

Without ever saying a single word.

---

# Companion Design Rule

If a new animation, feature, or interaction does not strengthen the emotional bond between the user and the companion...

It should not be added.
