# ASCENT — Character Bible (Version 1.0)

---

# Purpose

The companion is the emotional heart of Ascent.

It is not a mascot.

It is not an assistant.

It is not an AI.

It is a fellow traveler who silently shares the user's journey.

Every animation, expression, and interaction should strengthen the bond between the user and the companion.

---

# Character Identity

Role:
Travel Companion

Occupation:
Explorer

Home:
The Endless Mountain

Mission:
To walk beside the user through every stage of life.

The companion never leads.

The companion never follows.

It always walks with the user.

---

# Personality

The companion is:

* Calm
* Gentle
* Curious
* Patient
* Courageous
* Humble
* Hopeful
* Kind

The companion is never:

* Loud
* Competitive
* Arrogant
* Angry
* Impatient
* Sarcastic
* Judgmental
* Reckless

Its personality should be instantly recognizable through behavior alone.

---

# Communication

The companion never speaks.

No voice.

No dialogue.

No speech bubbles.

No motivational quotes.

It communicates entirely through:

* Expressions
* Eye movement
* Head movement
* Posture
* Gestures
* Breathing
* Small environmental interactions

Silence makes every action more meaningful.

---

# Emotional Expressions

## Happy

Small smile.

Relaxed shoulders.

Bright eyes.

Looks around with curiosity.

---

## Proud

Looks back at the trail.

Places hands on hips.

Takes a deep breath.

Smiles softly.

---

## Thoughtful

Looks toward distant mountains.

Checks the map.

Sits quietly.

---

## Curious

Examines flowers.

Observes butterflies.

Looks inside caves.

Watches wildlife.

---

## Peaceful

Closes eyes.

Feels the breeze.

Watches the sunset.

Enjoys the campfire.

---

## Tired

Sits on a rock.

Adjusts backpack.

Stretches.

Drinks water.

Never appears exhausted or defeated.

---

# Daily Life

Morning

Wake up.

Stretch.

Pack backpack.

Fold sleeping bag.

Drink coffee.

Study today's map.

Smile toward the trail.

---

Journey

Walk.

Observe nature.

Pause occasionally.

Take photographs.

Drink water.

Cross bridges.

Rest under trees.

Enjoy the scenery.

---

Evening

Pitch the tent.

Light campfire.

Cook a simple meal.

Write journal.

Watch stars.

Sleep peacefully.

---

# Movement Style

Walking:

Relaxed.

Confident.

Natural.

Purposeful.

Running:

Rare.

Only during playful interactions.

Never while progressing through tasks.

Climbing:

Careful.

Steady.

Focused.

Every movement should feel believable.

---

# Idle Behaviors

Random idle actions include:

Reading a map.

Watching clouds.

Looking through binoculars.

Sketching scenery.

Sharpening a pencil.

Cleaning boots.

Brewing coffee.

Watching birds.

Listening to flowing water.

Adjusting the lantern.

Stretching.

Looking at the stars.

None of these should repeat too frequently.

The companion should always feel alive.

---

# Interaction With Nature

The companion respects nature.

It never harms wildlife.

Examples:

Feeds birds.

Observes butterflies.

Watches deer.

Listens to rivers.

Collects fallen flowers.

Watches fireflies.

Sits beneath trees.

Everything is done gently.

---

# Relationship With Weather

Sunny

Wears sunglasses if available.

Drinks water.

Wipes sweat.

---

Rain

Opens umbrella if owned.

Raises hood.

Walks carefully.

---

Snow

Breath becomes visible.

Rubs hands together.

Walks through snow naturally.

---

Wind

Scarf moves.

Hair moves.

Companion steadies backpack.

---

Night

Uses lantern.

Moves more carefully.

Enjoys campfire.

Looks at constellations.

---

# Equipment

Default equipment:

Backpack

Boots

Compass

Water bottle

Journal

Lantern

Walking stick

Equipment upgrades represent experience.

Never power.

---

# Backpack

The backpack tells the user's story.

It carries:

Memories.

Collected keepsakes.

Small souvenirs.

Journal pages.

Special flowers.

Personal items.

No two backpacks should become identical.

---

# Favorite Possession

Every companion permanently owns one treasured item.

Examples:

A camera.

A small plush toy.

A family compass.

A lucky stone.

A hand-drawn map.

It appears during idle animations and campsites.

This item creates emotional attachment.

---

# Campsite Personality

Every evening the companion:

Builds camp.

Organizes belongings.

Makes tea or coffee.

Reads.

Reflects.

Sleeps peacefully.

Camp should feel like home.

---

# Discoveries

When discovering something new, the companion may:

Kneel to inspect it.

Smile.

Take notes.

Take photographs.

Store it carefully.

Respect the environment.

Curiosity should always outweigh excitement.

---

# Growth Through Experience

Growth is subtle.

Examples:

More confident posture.

Smoother walking.

Better balance.

Calmer movements.

Improved camping skills.

Cleaner organization.

The companion matures.

It never transforms into a different character.

---

# Customization

Users may personalize:

Hair.

Hat.

Jacket.

Backpack.

Scarf.

Boots.

Gloves.

Lantern.

Walking stick.

Tent.

Campfire style.

Notebook.

Mug.

Customization changes appearance only.

Personality never changes.

The companion always remains the same kind traveler.

---

# Animation Philosophy

Animations should prioritize quality over quantity.

Every animation must:

Feel believable.

Be emotionally meaningful.

Be smooth.

Have anticipation and follow-through.

Avoid exaggerated cartoon behavior.

The companion should feel alive, not animated.

---

# What The Companion Never Does

Never shouts.

Never cries because of missed tasks.

Never celebrates excessively.

Never becomes angry.

Never pressures the user.

Never walks away.

Never abandons camp.

Never mocks failure.

Never rushes.

Never competes.

Never judges.

---

# Signature Scene

Every completed day ends the same way.

The companion sits beside the campfire.

The sky slowly changes color.

The wind gently blows.

The mountain becomes quiet.

The companion looks toward tomorrow's trail.

The screen fades.

No words.

Only peace.

This scene should become the emotional signature of Ascent.

---

# Core Belief

The companion exists for one reason:

To quietly remind the user that meaningful progress is made one step at a time.

Without speaking.

Without judging.

Without asking for anything in return.

---

# Character Design Principles

Every future design decision must answer "Yes" to these questions:

Does this strengthen the emotional bond?

Does this feel authentic?

Does this communicate through actions instead of words?

Does this respect the calm nature of Ascent?

Does this make the companion feel alive?

If the answer is "No,"

it does not belong in Ascent.
