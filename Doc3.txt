# ASCENT — World Bible (Version 1.0)

---

# Purpose

The world of Ascent is not merely a background.

It is a living reflection of the user's journey.

Every mountain, trail, sunrise, snowfall, bird, flower, and campsite should remind the user that progress is beautiful.

The world should always feel calm, peaceful, inspiring, and full of hope.

---

# World Philosophy

The mountain is alive.

It remembers.

It grows with the user.

It never judges.

It patiently waits.

Every time the user opens Ascent, it should feel like returning to a familiar place.

---

# The Endless Mountain

The mountain has no final summit.

Every peak reveals another.

Growth never ends.

Progress is measured by altitude rather than levels.

The mountain symbolizes lifelong personal growth.

---

# Daily Expedition

Every day is a new expedition.

The companion begins from yesterday's campsite.

Today's tasks become today's trail.

Every completed task moves the companion one meaningful step forward.

At the end of the day:

If today's route is completed:

* Reach campsite
* Build camp
* Relax
* Watch the sunset
* Sleep

If today's route is incomplete:

* Camp wherever the journey paused
* Continue tomorrow from the same place

No progress is ever lost.

# Campsites

A campsite is the reward for today's effort.

Every completed day ends with peace.

Possible campsite objects:

* Tent
* Campfire
* Sleeping bag
* Lantern
* Wooden bench
* Backpack
* Coffee mug
* Compass
* Plants
* Small flags

The campsite slowly grows over months and years.

It becomes the user's second home.

Grass moves.

Clouds drift.

Water flows.

Snow falls.

# Hidden Discoveries

Sometimes the mountain quietly reveals surprises.

Examples:

* Ancient journal pages
* Crystal caves
* Forgotten camps
* Rare flowers
* Hidden viewpoints
* Stone statues
* Wooden shrines
* Old bridges
* Secret trails

These discoveries reward exploration rather than speed.

---

# Journal

Every completed day becomes a memory.

The companion writes one small journal entry.

Examples:

"Today's waterfall was beautiful."

"The wind was stronger today."

"We rested beneath the old pine."

"Tomorrow we'll continue."

The journal becomes the story of the user's life.

---

# Emotional Goal

Every time someone opens Ascent they should think:

"I'm home."

Every time they close Ascent they should think:

"I'm looking forward to tomorrow's climb."

---

# World Rules

The world never punishes.

The world never rushes.

The world never becomes chaotic.

The world always feels welcoming.

The world always reflects hope.

---

# Final Principle

The mountain is not where the journey happens.

The mountain IS the journey.
